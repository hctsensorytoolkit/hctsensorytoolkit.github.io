About Harbor Children's Therapy

Harbor Children’s Therapy is a private, therapist-owned occupational and physical therapy clinic that provides diagnostic evaluation and treatment for children age birth through 21 and their families. They offer specialized treatment in neurodevelopmental pediatrics and sensory integration, and provides therapy onsite at our clinic and consultation in both public and private school environments.

The owner of Harbor Children’s Therapy, Lois Kramer, is a licensed occupational therapist and brings over 30 years of experience to this clinic.

Harbor Children’s Therapy is committed to providing dependable, high quality, cutting-edge therapy to the Western Washington Community. Our goal is to help children reach their full developmental potential in the home, in school, and in social interactions.

About the Toolkit

This tool draws on existing sensory integration theory and the clinical expertise of current practitioners in this field.

The aim is to create a space where families can find resources that will enhance strong relationships through better understanding of child and parent sensory needs.

We hope that you will explore the many relationship pairings that may reflect the interaction between you and your child and make use of the resources suggested in a way that best fits your family.

About Us

The online sensory toolkit was developed students at the University of Washington pursuing a Master’s degree in occupational therapy in partnership with Harbor Children’s Therapy.

  • Annie Brandjord, OTS is a Seattle native with a background in public health and research. She has a keen interest in baking, trail running, and exploring the Pacific NW.
  • Lauren Eykholt, OTS is also a Seattle native and received her BA in public health and psychology from UW. She has experience working with children on social development, and her interests outside of school include art and hiking.
  • Hilary Figgs, OTS was raised in the San Juan Islands and has worked in infant and maternal health and international development. In her spare time, she enjoys houseplants, biking, and drawing.
  • Janet M. Powell, Ph.D., OTR/L, FAOTA worked as faculty advisor for this project. She is the Head of the Division of Occupational Therapy at the University of Washington with experience working as an OT in pediatrics and adult neurorehabilitation.
